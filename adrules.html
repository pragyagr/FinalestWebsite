Rules and Procedure for Admission



For Admission to the First Year of the Bachelors Degree Course

1. Pre-registration is a must for getting admission.

2. An applicant must have passed Senior School Certificate (10+ 2) or the Indian School Certificate (12years) or an Examination recognized as equivalent by University of Delhi.

3. There is no minimum age bar for admission to undergraduate courses.

4. Gap year(s) would be no bar for purpose of admission to the undergraduate course.

5. The cut off percentage for admission to each course will be put up on the college notice board/website on dates as per programme notified by the Delhi University. No individual intimation regarding admission will be sent.

6. After declaration of the Cut-off lists, eligible applicants should present themselves in person for admission. The applicant should bring the following documents:

a. 5 unattested, recent passport size photographs.
b. Original Certificates • Certificate and marksheet of class X • Marksheet of class XII • Character Certificate and Provisional Certificate from the institution last attended • For outstation candidates transfer and migration certificate.
c. Two sets of attested photocopies of the above certificates. Self attested documents will be accepted. If any false attestation or falsification of records is detected, admission will be cancelled and a case will be instituted against the applicant in accordance with the University rules.
d. Scheduled Caste (SC) / Scheduled Tribe (ST) / Other Backward Classes (OBC) applicants should attach a Certificate to the effect in their own name. SC/ST/OBC will be subjected to the verification of caste certificate by appropriate authority. If the certificate is found fake, the admission will be considered invalid. The differently-abled applicants should attach the latest certificate of disability not exceeding six months prior to the date of admission. 3% seats in all under graduate and post graduate will be reserved for candidates having minimum 40% physical disabilities.

7. Documents to be submitted on Orientation Day (i.e. 20th of July 2015)
a) As per the requirements of ordinance XV-B, it is mandatory for every student to submit an anti ragging affidavits (the format of which are given as Annexures I and II) on notarized non-judicial stamp paper of Rs. 50/-.
b) Two undertaking forms each duly signed by the student and parent should be submitted to the Teacher-In-charge of the concerned department.

8. Admission to all courses are routed through various committees constituted for the purpose. Applicants are requested not to approach the Principal in connection with admissions.

9. No student who qualifies on the basis of the percentage indicated in the cut-off list shall be denied admission provided she pays the fees by the stipulated date and time.

10. Every student shall be required to sign a declaration to submit herself to the disciplinary jurisdiction of the Principal, Vice-Chancellor and other authorities in accordance with the Statutes and Ordinances of University of Delhi.

11. All admissions are subject to the approval of University of Delhi.



For Admission to M.A. Part-I

1. An applicant must have passed the Bachelors Degree Examination (10 + 2 + 3).

2. There is no minimum age bar for admission to post-graduate courses in the University and its Colleges.

3. The previous academic record should be of high standard.

4. For admission to M.A., students must also apply directly to the appropriate Faculty in the University of Delhi for registration. No decision regarding admission to the College will be taken prior to the preliminary selection.



Foreign Students

Admission of foreign students is dealt with by the Deputy Dean (Foreign Students), Conference Centre, University of Delhi, Delhi-110007. Email: dydean_fs@du.ac.in. No foreign student is admitted directly by the college without a letter from the Foreign Student's Advisor, University of Delhi, regarding her eligibility and clearance for her holding student’s visa. As per University rules, an amount of Indian Rupees equivalent of US$ 100 per year will be paid by every foreign student to the college along with fee.



Hostel Admission

1. Students seeking hostel accommodation should indicate so in the admission form of the college and should submit a separate hostel application form (attached to the Hostel Prospectus) on the date of declaration of the cut-off lists of hostel. It will be displayed on college website/college notice/ display board/hostel notice board.

2. Immediately after the process of admission to the college is completed with the payment of fees, students desirous of seeking admission in the Hostel and those who have applied for admission, are required to register themselves at the Hostel Office and submit the counter foil of their hostel application form and receipt of fees deposited in the college. For details refer to the college Hostel prospectus 2015-16.

Note: The Students are also required to visit the University and College Websites from time to time for any updates

ADMISSION ON THE BASIS OF EXCELLENCE IN EXTRA-CURRICULAR ACTIVITIES


Admission to various courses in the academic year 2015-16 on the basis of extracurricular activity (ECA) will be strictly as per University of Delhi guidelines. Under these guidelines ECA committee has been constituted which will be recommending students for admission on the basis of extracurricular activity.

Students seeking admission under the ECA category should fill the ECA form obtainable from the college. ECA entitles students to a concession of 5% in the cut- off percentage of the second list of the college in the relevant course of study. There are a limited number of seats available under this category and only those with outstanding achievement should apply.

Students are admitted on the basis of trials conducted by the college. Each student is compulsorily required to appear for trials and can appear for trial for maximum two activities. Students are required to bring their own equipment, materials, sound accompaniments, costumes, musical instruments, etc. as may be required for the trials. Academic performance will carry weightage in the selection process Admissions under the ECA category take place usually after the second cut-off list of the general admissions. The Complete schedule for the trials will be notified on the college website (www.dr.du.ac.in) and notice board.

Students must have documentary evidence of following over the past three years:
1) Participation in the said activity/event at inter- school, inter-district, state, national level etc.
2) Formal training in the said field from recognized bodies.
3) Proof of performances in public domain.
    Candidates appearing for the trials are requested to bring all original certificates/awards etc testifying the above.

    Following are the activities on the basis of which a candidate can seek admission under the ECA category :
1) Dramatics ( English/Hindi)
2) Fine Arts
3) Music (Vocal/Instrumental )
4) Debating (English/Hindi)
5) Dance (Classical/Western )
6) Creative writing (English/ Hindi)



Candidates are required to attach attested copies of the following, while filling in the form under the ECA category (original certificates for the same should be presented at the time of trials) :

1) Documents in support of achievements in ECA, on the basis of which admission is sought ( for last 3 years only ).
2) A letter of recommendation from the head/ principal/ officiating authority of the institution last attended, giving details of the activities in which the candidate has participated. This letter is not mandatory but desirable.
3) Statement of Purpose written by the candidate stating how can she contribute to the cultural life of the college and reasons for her choosing our college.
4) It is desirable if Portfolios are also attached especially for those applying under the category of fine arts.
5) Those applying under the category of performing arts are required to submit a recording of their performances in a CD.
6) Mark sheet on the basis of which the admission is sought.
7) Provisional/ pass certificate of last exam passed.
8) Conduct / character certificate from the institution last attended.
9) School Examination certificate (class x certificate), stating date of birth.
10) Transfer/ Migration certificate, if any. 11) Caste certificate (reserved categories - SC/ ST/ OBC)



Note: The college reserves the right not to make admissions under the category if no candidate is found suitable. The decision of the college regarding selections will be final and binding. No queries towards the same will be entertained. Students admitted under the ECA category are required to consistently participate in these activities.
